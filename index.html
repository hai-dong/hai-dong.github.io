Dr. Hai Dong is currently a Professor at the School of Computing Technologies, RMIT University, Melbourne, Australia. He is the Leader of the Systems and Security Group and Founding Director of the CloudTech-RMIT Green Cryptocurrency Joint Research Laboratory (GreenCryptoLab). Previously, he was a Vice-Chancellor's Research Fellow at RMIT University and a Curtin Research Fellow at Curtin University, Australia. He holds a PhD from Curtin University, a Bachelor's degree from Northeastern University, China, and a Graduate Certificate in Learning and Teaching. He has published a monograph and 150+ (including 100+ CORE-A*/A or JCR/SJR-Q1) research articles in international journals and conferences, such as AAAI, ACM MM, ASE, ESORICS, ICML, ICSOC, ICWS, TIFS, TMC, TOSEM, TSC, and TSE. He received the Best Paper Award at ICSOC 2016 and IEEE ICBC 2025, as well as the 2023 RMIT Award for Research Engagement and Impact - Industry Engagement in Graduate Research. He has secured 5 million+ research funding as a chief investigator from government sources (ARC, CRC, QNRF) and industry (ANZ, CloudTech, Telstra). He currently chairs the IEEE Computational Intelligence Society Task Force on Deep Edge Intelligence. He has served on the organising and program committees of 100+ international conferences, such as AAAI, CIKM, ICDM, ICSOC, ICWS, and WWW, and has been an invited reviewer for 50+ international journals, such as CSUR, TKDE, TOIT, and TSC. His primary research interests include: Service-Oriented Computing, Edge Intelligence, Blockchain, AI Security, Cyber Security, and Machine Learning. He is a Senior Member of the IEEE.
