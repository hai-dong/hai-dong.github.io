 Dr. Hai Dong is a senior lecturer (equiv. to associate professor in North America) at School of Computing Technologies in RMIT University, Melbourne, Australia. He is the leader of Smart Sensing and Services Research Area, the deputy director and co-founder of CloudTech-RMIT Green Cryptocurrency Joint Research Laboratory (GreenCryptoLab), and a member of RMIT Centre for Cyber Security Research and Innovation (CCSRI). Prior to this position, he served as a Vice-Chancellor's Research Fellow in RMIT University and a Curtin Research Fellow in Curtin University, Australia. He received a PhD from Curtin University and a Bachelor's degree from Northeastern University, China. He published a monograph and 110+ (65+ CORE-A*/A/SJR Top-10%) research articles in international journals and conferences, including CACM, CSUR, JCSS, TIE, TII, TMC, TSC, TSE, ICSOC, ICWS, etc. His publications receive 2300+ citations and H-Index of 26 according to Google Scholar statistics. He received the Best Research Paper Award in ICSOC 2016. He obtained 3 million+ research funding from government and industry. He currently serves as a chair for the IEEE Computational Intelligence Society Task Force on Deep Edge Intelligence. He served in the organising/program committees of 80+ international conferences, including ICSOC, ICWS, etc. He was invited to review for 50+ international journals, such as CSUR, TKDE, TOIT, TSC, etc. His primary research interests include: Service-Oriented Computing, Edge Computing, Blockchain, Cybersecurity, Machine Learning, Data Science, etc. He is a senior member of the IEEE and a member of the ACM.
